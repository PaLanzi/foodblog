Käsespätzle

Käsespätzle (Cheese noodles) A South German specialty that makes everyone feel happy around the table? Creamy cheese spaetzle! For our recipe you combine, among other things, Emmentaler with completely homemade "sparrows" as it is called in southern Germany and refine everything with aromatic roasted onions.

For the spaetzle we need: 250 g wheat flour, 4 egg ,1/2 salt, 50 g mountain cheese ,75 g Emmentaler cheese, grated, 2 onions 70 g butter

Ingredients Quantity Preparation
Wheat Flour 250g Put the flour in a bowl, make a well in the center
Eggs 4 add 4 Eggs into the bowl and 40 ml Water
Salt 1/2 big Spoon put the Salt into the bowl, keep attention not too much but not too less. Now stir everything into a fine dough.
Mountain Cheese 50g Beat the dough vigorously until it has a smooth, chewy consistency and falls off the spoon in thick drops. Add more water if necessary. Then let the dough rest for 15 minutes. Grate the mountain cheese and the Emmentaler
Emmentaler cheese, grated 75g mix the both cheese in an extra bowl
Onions , Butter 2 and 70g cut the onions, prepare a pan. Then put 50 g butter in a pan over medium heat and let it melt. Fry the onion slices in it until they are crispy and golden brown.
Final Step Now Final Step! Bring a saucepan of water to a boil. Use a spaetzle slicer - a perforated sheet with a container that is pushed back and forth - to drip part of the spaetzle dough into bubbling boiling water. Let the freely swimming spaetzle soak in the water until they rise to the surface. Then carefully remove them with a slotted spoon. Repeat the process until all of the batter is used. Melt the remaining 20 g butter in two appropriately sized pans. Cover the bottom of the pan with the first layer of spaetzle and put a layer of cheese on top. Layer the spaetzle and cheese alternately in both pans until all the ingredients are used up. Finally, pour the fried onions over the cheese noodles.
